WHAT IS NEXUS?

Nexus is a home for all of my projects, displaying the skills I've learned and the things I've made with them. The first project is Nexus itself. I've never been taught HTML, nor is it covered in any classes I've taken. It's not a requirement to graduate or something that anyone is forcing me to do. It's a promise that I'll motivate myself to make great things. In many way Nexus acts as a minimum viable product for a hub for projects. While it'll impress few it acts as a definitive mark of what I've done and a great place to tell others what I plan to do.

Nexus will display formats in a format that answers "What is it" or "What does it do", "What does it demonstrate", and "What's planned"

WHAT DOES NEXUS DEMONSTRATE?

HTML, CSS, and web design are obvious answers, and nexus does clearly show that I can work my way around VS code. But beyond the obvious it also shows that I can set goals, hold myself accountable, and teach myself. While the skills to self-direct personal projects may seem basic it's important to show I have them.
